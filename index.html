Piano Transcription Onset F-1 Random Gaussian Shift Listening Test

What it is and How to do it

To understand the onset F-1 score, I will randomly generate the number using a zero-mean normal distribution with a different standard deviation (std) and shift the note accordingly for each test. The test contains four different segments from same piece that falls under a specific range of F-1 score. I won't share the F-1 score between the reference section and randomly shifted section to eleminate the bias. One has to listen to the pair of audios and shares thought on whether they sound same. This survey will helps us to find the threshold onset F-1 score which the listener starts to recognize the differences.

The Main Instructions

This experiment is an attempt to determine the point at which small changes in the timings of notes become objectionable to listeners and performers. You will be presented with multiple sets of four pairs of musical excerpt. The first member of each pair is the original performance. The second member of each pair is an approximation to that performance.

Please indicate whether you consider the second excerpt is an acceptable approximation to the first excerpt either as a listener or as a performer, as you had indicated above. You can use link(Google Forms) to submit the your answer (right click the link and open in new tab) or email me dhtheuno@gmail.com with “acceptable" or “not acceptable" answer for each segment.

This information will help us determine the point at which listeners begin to recognizes differences in the details of musical timing. Thank you for participating in this experiment!
